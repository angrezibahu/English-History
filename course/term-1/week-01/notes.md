# Week 1 — After Rome: Britain in the 5th Century

When the last Roman troops left Britain around **410 AD**, they left behind a
province that had to fend for itself. This week sets the scene for everything
that follows.

## Key themes

- The withdrawal of Roman administration and the collapse of the money economy.
- The arrival of Angles, Saxons, and Jutes — migration, not simply invasion.
- How later writers such as *Gildas* and *Bede* shaped the story we inherited.

> "The Romans, therefore, left the island... telling them to look after
> themselves." — Bede, paraphrased

## Listen for

While you listen to the lecture, keep three questions in mind:

1. Why did Roman Britain unravel so quickly compared to Gaul?
2. What does the archaeology tell us that the written sources do not?
3. How much of "the Dark Ages" is real, and how much is a gap in our records?
