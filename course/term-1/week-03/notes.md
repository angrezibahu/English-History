# Week 3 — The Viking Age

From the raid on **Lindisfarne in 793** to the settlement of the Danelaw, the
Norse reshaped England over two centuries — as raiders, then as settlers, and
finally as kings.

## From raids to conquest

1. **Raiding (793–850)** — fast coastal strikes on monasteries and towns.
2. **The Great Heathen Army (865)** — a shift from plunder to conquest.
3. **Settlement** — the *Danelaw*, where Norse law and language took root.

## Alfred and the survival of Wessex

Wessex alone withstood the onslaught. After near-defeat at Chippenham, Alfred's
victory at **Edington (878)** turned the tide. His reforms — burhs, a reorganised
army, and a revival of learning — laid the groundwork for a single English kingdom.

*Why did Wessex survive when Northumbria, East Anglia, and Mercia fell? That is
the central question for this week's lecture.*
