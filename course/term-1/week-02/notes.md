# Week 2 — The Anglo-Saxon Kingdoms

By the 7th century the patchwork of small territories had consolidated into a
handful of powerful kingdoms — the *Heptarchy*. This week we trace how power
moved between them.

## The major kingdoms

| Kingdom      | Heartland            | Known for                       |
|--------------|----------------------|---------------------------------|
| Northumbria  | North-east England   | Learning, Lindisfarne, Bede     |
| Mercia       | The Midlands         | Offa and his great dyke         |
| Wessex       | The south-west       | Survival, and eventual unity    |
| Kent         | The south-east       | The first Christian conversions |

## Turning points

- The **conversion to Christianity** from 597, arriving from two directions:
  Roman missionaries in the south, Irish monks in the north.
- The **Synod of Whitby (664)**, which aligned the English church with Rome.
- The slow rise of **Mercian** dominance under Æthelbald and Offa.

Listen to the lecture for the story of how a frontier society built durable
institutions — kingship, law codes, and the church — that outlasted it.
