# Week 1 — 1066 and the Norman Conquest

A single year changed England more than any other. Three claimants, three
battles, and one arrow at Hastings redirected the country's language, landholding,
and ruling class for centuries.

## The contenders

- **Harold Godwinson** — crowned in January 1066, the last Anglo-Saxon king.
- **Harald Hardrada** — the Norwegian king, defeated at Stamford Bridge.
- **William of Normandy** — victorious at Hastings on 14 October 1066.

## What actually changed

- A new **Norman aristocracy** replaced almost the entire English elite.
- The **Domesday Book (1086)** recorded who held what, in extraordinary detail.
- **Castles** appeared across the landscape as instruments of control.
- **French** became the language of the court and law for generations.

This term opens by asking how much of England was remade — and how much simply
carried on under new management.
